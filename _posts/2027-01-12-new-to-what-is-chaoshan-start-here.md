## The Cultural Context

In the misty mountains of eastern Guangdong, a tea tradition has flourished for nearly a thousand years. Phoenix Dan Cong (凤凰单丛) is not just a beverage — it is a living cultural heritage, recognized by UNESCO as Intangible Cultural Heritage.

If you are new to **what is chaoshan**, welcome. You are about to enter one of the most rewarding tea traditions in the world. This guide assumes no prior knowledge — just curiosity and a willingness to learn.

Phoenix Dan Cong can seem intimidating at first. The names are in Chinese. The brewing seems complicated. The prices vary wildly. But strip away the complexity, and what you find is simply one of the most delicious teas on earth, made by people who care deeply about their craft.

## What Is Phoenix Dan Cong?

Dan Cong is a type of oolong tea grown on Phoenix Mountain in Guangdong Province, southern China. The name translates as "single bush" — and that is the key. Each batch of Dan Cong comes from a single tea bush, not a blended plantation. This means every Dan Cong has a unique character.

Think of it like wine. Most tea is blended from many plants, like a supermarket wine. Dan Cong is from one plant, like a single-vineyard wine. That is why it is special — and why it costs more.

## Why People Love It

Three reasons:

- **Aroma**: Dan Cong is the most aromatic tea in the world. Some varieties smell like honey, flowers, or fruit — without anything added.

- **Depth**: A single brewing session can last 10 infusions, with the flavor changing each time.

- **Story**: Every Dan Cong has a name, a history, and a specific bush it came from. You are drinking a place, not a product.

## Your First Brewing Session

Here is the simplest way to brew Dan Cong at home:

- Get a small teapot or gaiwan (80-120ml)

- Use 5 grams of tea leaves

- Heat water to 95°C (just below boiling)

- Rinse the leaves with hot water, discard immediately

- Steep for 5 seconds, pour into a cup

- For each next infusion, add 5 seconds

- Enjoy 8-12 infusions

That is it. Do not overthink it. The first cup will teach you more than any guide.

## Which Dan Cong To Try First?

For your first Dan Cong, choose **Honey Orchid (Milan Xiang)**. It is the most beginner-friendly variety — sweet, floral, and forgiving of brewing mistakes. You cannot go wrong with it.

Avoid starting with rare or expensive varieties. The nuance of high-end Dan Cong is lost on beginners, and the cost will make you nervous — which ruins the experience. Start simple, learn the basics, and work your way up.

## Common Beginner Questions

### Why is it so expensive?
Authentic Dan Cong is rare. Each bush produces only a few kilograms per year, and the processing is labor-intensive. You are paying for craftsmanship, not marketing.

### Why does my tea taste bitter?
Two reasons: water too hot, or steeping too long. Use 95°C water and steep for 5 seconds on the first infusion. If it is still bitter, reduce the leaf quantity.

### Do I need fancy equipment?
No. A simple gaiwan and small cups are enough. Fancy teapots enhance the experience but are not required.

### Can I drink it every day?
Yes. Dan Cong has moderate caffeine (about 40-60mg per cup). Most people enjoy it in the morning or afternoon.
